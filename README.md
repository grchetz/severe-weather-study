## Educational Context
Severe weather refers to any dangerous meteorological phenomena with the potential to cause damage, serious social disruption, or loss of human life. They include thunderstorms (with damaging winds, large hail, or tornadoes), tropical cyclones (hurricanes, typhoons), blizzards, ice storms, heat waves, cold waves, or dense fog. Severe weather differs from extreme weather, which describes unusual weather events that stand out from the normal climate of a specific place at a specific time.

Types of severe weather:
- Thunderstorms: Produce lightning, heavy rain, hail, strong winds, and tornadoes.
- Tornadoes: Violent rotating columns of air.
- Tropical cyclones: Large-scale low-pressure systems with sustained winds over 74 mph.
- Winter storms: Blizzards, ice storms, heavy snow.

Impacts: Severe weather causes billions in damage annually and significant loss of life worldwide. Early warning systems and preparedness mitigate risks.
